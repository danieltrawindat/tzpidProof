\begin{abstract}
This closing paper pays the series' two outstanding debts: unification and cosmological closure. The first half develops the third canonical gold spine, Topological Unification. Distinct interaction sectors are unified not by a larger gauge group but by a single topological obstruction. A connection curvature $F = dA + A \wedge A$ (ID9342) builds a Chern--Simons action $S_{CS} = \int_M A \wedge dA + \Gamma_{\mathrm{acoustic}}$ (ID8480); its quantized Chern number $C_1 = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{BZ} F \cdot dS \in \mathbb{Z}$ (ID8931) and the Gauss linking number $Lk$ of poloidal/toroidal linkage (ID0643) assemble one topological charge $\Omega_{\mathrm{top}} = C_1(T^2) + \pi\, w(T^2) + \log \Pi_\pi$ (ID9892), whose non-vanishing is the unified obstruction (ID9176) issuing in the Trawin topology unified field equation $h_{\mu\nu} = A\, \psi^* \partial_\mu \partial_\nu \psi$ (ID5773): geometry as the second-derivative shadow of a wavefunction. The guardrails certify Chern quantization, the implication $CS(A) \neq 0 \Rightarrow \Omega_{\mathrm{top}} \neq 0$, and the decomposition of the unified charge. The second half closes the cosmology: in a closed $S^3$ universe the Hubble rate is the breathing of the enclosure radius, $H = \dot{R}/R$ with $\dot{V}/V = 3H_0 \approx 0.21$--$0.23$ per Gyr, and the cosmological constant is the same radius read as vacuum tension, $\rho_\Lambda = (\hbar c/R^4)(\Phi_0/\Phi_{\mathrm{total}}) \sim H_0^2 \approx 6 \times 10^{-10}$ J\,m$^{-3}$ (ID0400/ID1392), verified at $\Lambda = 3\Omega_\Lambda H_0^2/c^2 = 1.09$--$1.30 \times 10^{-52}$ m$^{-2}$ with dark-energy length $\lambda_\Lambda \approx 85$--$88\ \mu$m. The $10^{123}$ cosmological-constant problem becomes the radius hierarchy $(R_\Lambda/\ell_P)^4$ between the registry's Planck-radius and Hubble-radius brackets (ID2462/ID2453); breathing drives matter creation through Paper VII's threshold; and the testable closed-geometry model (ID0388) carries curvature radius $R_c \approx 229$--$324$ Gly with $\sin\chi$ angular-diameter signatures, low-$\ell$ CMB cutoffs, and redshift drift. The series closes where it opened: the enclosure is the stage, the mechanism, the signature, the engine, the force, the audit, the source---and, finally, the unification.

\section{Introduction: what remains to be unified}
\label{sec:intro}

Seven papers have each delivered a piece: the enclosure stage (I), the locking mechanism (II), the holonomy signature (III), the gyromagnetic engine (IV), accumulated gravity (V), the residual audit (VI), and the matter source (VII). What remains is the claim that gives a theory of everything its name: that the pieces are one thing seen seven ways, and that the seeing has a single mathematical carrier.

The series' answer is topological, and the choice is principled. Symmetry-based unification (grand unified groups, supersymmetry) merges interactions by embedding their gauge groups; it has produced beautiful structure and, to date, no confirmed proton decay or sparticle. The registry's third gold spine takes the other road: interactions are unified by what their field configurations \emph{cannot avoid being}---by topology. Helicity (Paper IV) already unified fluid and magnetic movement through linking; the comma (Paper III) already unified music and geometry through holonomy; the reciprocal flip (Papers I--III) already unified criticality, orbits, and intervals through one inversion. Each was a topological invariant doing cross-domain work. This paper assembles the invariants themselves into a single charge.

The second debt is cosmological. Paper I promised that the outermost enclosure is the universe; Papers V and VII parked the cosmological constant and the breathing engine pending closure. Here both are redeemed: $H_0$ and $\Lambda$ as two readouts of one breathing radius, with the numbers computed and cross-checked against the registry.

\section{Limits of the construction}
\label{sec:limits}

Honest closure requires stating what this paper has not done. First, the obstruction theorem certifies an implication ($CS(A) \neq 0 \Rightarrow \Omega_{\mathrm{top}} \neq 0$), not a classification: the spine establishes that the sectors share a non-trivial charge, but the full classification of which sector couplings are forced by which values of $\Omega_{\mathrm{top}}$ remains open registry work. Second, the unified field equation \eqref{eq:unifiedfield} is exhibited at linearized level ($h_{\mu\nu}$, not the full metric); its nonlinear completion, and the demonstration that it reproduces \eqref{eq:friedmann} as its homogeneous limit, is the natural next gold spine. Third, the cosmological identification of Section~\ref{sec:breathing} shares the standard burden of all closed-universe proposals: current data prefer flatness at $|\Omega_K| \lesssim 0.002$, and the framework lives in the still-allowed sliver---the Di Valentino--Melchiorri--Silk closed-universe reading of Planck lensing and the DESI evolving-dark-energy hints keep the sliver interesting, but a decisive flatness measurement at the $10^{-4}$ level would close it. Fourth, the Hubble-tension correlation prediction (Section~\ref{sec:demarcation}, item iv) is the framework's nearest-term exposure: it requires no new instruments, only a joint statistical treatment of existing $H_0$ and $\Lambda$ inference chains, and a null correlation would wound the two-readouts claim at its center. The series prefers these exposures explicit: a unification whose failure modes are listed is a scientific proposal; one without them is a story.
