## Purpose

This page explains the intended licensing posture for the TZPIDProof repository in plain language. The project is intended to be open for academic inspection, citation, peer review, teaching, and non-commercial research, while preserving a commercial-sharing term for technology implementation.

## CC40 / CC BY 4.0-Style Academic Attribution Layer

For papers, documentation, explanatory prose, tables, metadata, registry descriptions, proof notes, and scholarly review material, the intended academic permission is:

- You may read, cite, quote, redistribute, and build scholarly analysis from the material.
- You may use the material in non-commercial academic, educational, archival, and review contexts.
- You must attribute Daniel Alexander Trawin as creator.
- You should preserve citations, DOI links, ORCID, source-truth metadata, and repository links where practical.
- You should state if you modified, summarized, translated, or excerpted the material.

Recommended attribution:

```text
Daniel Alexander Trawin, TZPIDProof: Proof, provenance, and certificate package
for the TZPID registry and gold-spine proof architecture, 2026.
ORCID: 0009-0001-4630-3715
Repository: https://github.com/danieltrawindat/tzpidProof.git
```

Important clarification: Creative Commons licenses themselves do not create a 50 percent commercial revenue-sharing term. The 50 percent term belongs to the project-specific RL50 commercial layer below.

## RL50 Commercial Technology Layer

The project uses the term **RL50** to mean the following intended commercial rule:

> Commercial exploitation of project-derived technology, methods, systems,
> protocols, implementations, products, engineering designs, or monetized
> deployments requires a separate agreement with Daniel Alexander Trawin, with
> the default project term reserving 50 percent of net commercial gains arising
> from that exploitation.

Commercial use includes, but is not limited to:

- selling a product or service based on the technology;
- embedding the technology in a paid platform;
- sublicensing or reselling project-derived systems;
- commercial deployment of devices, protocols, algorithms, or engineering designs;
- raising revenue from an implementation whose value substantially depends on the project material.

The RL50 layer is intended to protect the technology side of the work while keeping the academic proof, review, and citation side open.

## What Is Open Without Separate Commercial Permission

Without a separate agreement, the following uses are intended to be allowed with attribution:

- academic reading and citation;
- peer review, commentary, and critique;
- non-commercial replication of calculations or formal checks;
- classroom or research-group discussion;
- archival redistribution for scholarly preservation;
- non-commercial scripts or notebooks used to verify the published claims.

## What Requires Permission

The following require prior written permission or a separate commercial agreement:

- monetized deployment;
- productization;
- paid consulting deliverables based on the technology;
- commercial software/platform integration;
- patent filings, hardware products, or device claims based substantially on project-derived technology;
- private sublicensing or resale.
