\subsection{Statement}

\subsection{Discussion}
The standard Friedmann–Lemaître–Robertson–Walker (FLRW) metric describes an expanding universe with scale factor $a(t)$. This interpretation retains the observational content of the usual Hubble law but alters the metaphoric language: recession speeds do not indicate motion through space but reflect the stretching of the space itself.
