\tzpsection{Canonical Statement}
The Trawinistic connection generalizes the Levi-Civita connection so that covariant differentiation remains meaningful in a manifold with a degenerate metric at the zero point. It agrees with ordinary geometry away from the anchor and acquires singular behavior only where the null sector requires it.

\tzpsection{Derivation Layer}
\paragraph{Primary Equation.}
\begin{equation}
\nabla^{T}g=0,
\qquad
T(\nabla^{T})=0
\quad
(x\neq p).
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Primary Derivation.}
Away from the TZP, the geometry should reduce to ordinary metric-compatible differential geometry. Therefore the Trawinistic connection $\nabla^{T}$ must satisfy metric compatibility:

\begin{equation}
\nabla^{T}g=0 .
\end{equation}

It must also be torsion-free away from the degenerate point:

\begin{equation}
T(\nabla^{T})=0,
\qquad x\neq p .
\end{equation}

When $\det(g)\neq 0$, the unique connection satisfying metric compatibility and zero torsion is the Levi-Civita connection. Hence:

\begin{equation}
\nabla^{T}=\nabla^{LC},
\qquad x\neq p .
\end{equation}

At $p$, where $\det(g)|_p=0$, $\nabla^{T}$ is extended by the rule that null vectors are annihilated or stabilized under the degenerate structure.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The Trawinistic connection preserves ordinary geometry away from $p$ while extending the differential structure into the TZP degeneracy.
