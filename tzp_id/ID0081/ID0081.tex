\tzpsection{Derivation Layer}
\paragraph{Primary Statement.}
Classical emergence occurs when decoherence suppresses interference and pointer states evolve according to effective classical equations derived from the Trawinistic Hamiltonian.

\paragraph{Primary Condition.}
\begin{equation}
\rho_{ij}(t)\rightarrow 0
\qquad
(i\neq j).
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Primary Derivation.}
Let $\rho$ be a density matrix written in a pointer-state basis $\{|i\rangle\}$.  
The off-diagonal entries
\begin{equation}
\rho_{ij}
=
\langle i|\rho|j\rangle,
\qquad i\neq j,
\end{equation}
encode quantum interference between distinct pointer states.

Classical behavior emerges when these off-diagonal terms decay rapidly:
\begin{equation}
\rho_{ij}(t)\rightarrow 0
\qquad
(i\neq j).
\end{equation}

The remaining diagonal entries behave as an effective classical probability distribution:
\begin{equation}
p_i(t)=\rho_{ii}(t).
\end{equation}

Thus the quantum state becomes approximately classical in the pointer basis:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\rho(t)
\longrightarrow
\sum_i p_i(t)|i\rangle\langle i|.
}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
Classical behavior is obtained when interference is suppressed and the pointer-state probabilities dominate the observable dynamics.
