\paragraph{Canonical Statement.}
- **Energy source:** stars have internal heat from nuclear fusion (or gravitational contraction for young stars), not external surface heating. - **Medium:** plasma (charged particles) — so **magnetic fields** and electromagnetic forces (MHD) are crucial. - **Transport mechanisms:** radiative transfer can dominate over conduction. Where radiation can carry the luminosity, the star is **radiative**; where it cannot, the star becomes **convective**. - **Gravity is dominant:** gravity sets pressure and density stratification; pressure scale height \textbackslash{}(H\_p = \textbackslash{}frac\{kT\}\{\textbackslash{}mu m\_p g\}\textbackslash{}) often sets convective cell scales. - **Vacuum outside:** radiative losses go into cold space (≈2.7 K), but those losses aren’t the driver of internal convection — internal heat generation and opacity determine where convection occurs. - **Dimensionless criteria for convection (Schwarzschild criterion):** convection occurs where the actual temperature gradient exceeds the adiabatic gradient.
