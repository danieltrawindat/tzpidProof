\tzpsection{Derivation Layer}
\paragraph{Primary Statement.}

Persistence-diagram entropy is obtained by converting topological lifetimes into a normalized probability distribution and measuring the information carried by that distribution.

\paragraph{Primary Derivation.}

Let
\[
D=\{(b_i,d_i)\}_{i=1}^{N}
\]
be a persistence diagram with lifetimes
\[
\ell_i=d_i-b_i,\qquad \ell_i\ge 0.
\]
The total persistence mass is
\[
L_D=\sum_{i=1}^{N}\ell_i.
\]
When \(L_D>0\), define normalized persistence weights by
\[
p_i=\frac{\ell_i}{L_D},\qquad \sum_{i=1}^{N}p_i=1.
\]
The persistence entropy is therefore
\begin{equation}
S_D=-\sum_{i=1}^{N}p_i\log p_i.
\end{equation}
To include Wasserstein sensitivity, let \(D'\) be a comparison diagram and let
\[
W_p(D,D')=\left(\inf_{\gamma:D\to D'}\sum_{x\in D}\|x-\gamma(x)\|^{p}\right)^{1/p}
\]
be the \(p\)-Wasserstein distance between matched diagrams. A normalized stability score may be written as
\[
\mathcal{S}_{W}(D,D')=\frac{S_D}{1+W_p(D,D')}.
\]

\paragraph{Wolfram Form.}

weights = lifetimes/Total[lifetimes];
SD = -Total[weights*Log[weights]];
SW = SD/(1 + Wp[D, Dp, p]);
