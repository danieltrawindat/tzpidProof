\paragraph{Primary Equation.}

\paragraph{Primary Derivation.}
From mode quantization, a confined field can be expanded as:

\begin{equation}
\phi=\sum_n \phi_n .
\end{equation}

Boundary geometry modifies the amplitude or phase of each mode through coefficients $b_n(\partial\Omega)$.

The boundary-response operator is therefore:

\begin{equation}
\boxed{
B_{\partial\Omega}\phi
=
\sum_n b_n(\partial\Omega)\,\phi_n .
}
\end{equation}

The coefficient naturally decomposes into geometric, topological, and curvature components:

\begin{equation}
b_n
=
b_n^{\mathrm{geom}}
+
b_n^{\mathrm{top}}
+
b_n^{\mathrm{curv}} .
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
Boundaries actively reshape quantum fields by reweighting their confined mode expansion.
