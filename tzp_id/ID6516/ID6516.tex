\paragraph{Canonical Statement.}
\# 3) Dynamics — barotropic vorticity equation (compact) A minimal dynamical equation that contains Coriolis, advection, wave–vortex interactions and viscous dissipation is the barotropic vorticity equation on a rotating sphere: \textbackslash{}[ \textbackslash{}boxed\{\textbackslash{}frac\{\textbackslash{}partial \textbackslash{}zeta\}\{\textbackslash{}partial t\} + J(\textbackslash{}Psi,\textbackslash{}zeta+f) \textbackslash{};=\textbackslash{}; \textbackslash{}nu\textbackslash{}nabla\textasciicircum{}2\_\{\textbackslash{}!s\}\textbackslash{}zeta + F(\textbackslash{}theta,\textbackslash{}lambda,t)\} \textbackslash{}] where \textbackslash{}(J\textbackslash{}) is the horizontal Jacobian (advection), \textbackslash{}(\textbackslash{}nu\textbackslash{}) is a kinematic viscosity/eddy-diffusivity, and \textbackslash{}(F\textbackslash{}) is external forcing (e.g., wave forcing, mass input). This equation naturally lets the dipole interact with Rossby waves through the \textbackslash{}(J(\textbackslash{}Psi,\textbackslash{}zeta+f)\textbackslash{}) term.

\[
\boxed{\frac{\partial \zeta}{\partial t} + J(\Psi,\zeta+f) \;=\; \nu\nabla^2_{\!s}\zeta + F(\theta,\lambda,t)}
