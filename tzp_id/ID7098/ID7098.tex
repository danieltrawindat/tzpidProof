\paragraph{Canonical Statement.}
- Lab large-source strategy: suppose source mass \textbackslash{}(M\_s=10\textasciicircum{}4\textbackslash{}) kg, test mass difference Δm = 10 kg, and local field \textbackslash{}(g\_s\textbackslash{}) from that source at test location is, say, \textbackslash{}(10\textasciicircum{}\{-5\}\textbackslash{}) m/s² (depends strongly on geometry). Then for α=1: \textbackslash{}[ \textbackslash{}Delta a \textbackslash{}approx g\_s\textbackslash{}frac\{\textbackslash{}Delta m\}\{M\_s\} = 10\textasciicircum{}\{-5\}\textbackslash{}cdot\textbackslash{}frac\{10\}\{10\textasciicircum{}4\}=10\textasciicircum{}\{-8\}\textbackslash{} \textbackslash{}text\{m/s\}\textasciicircum{}2, \textbackslash{}] which is measurable with good instrumentation. So a local source experiment can amplify the effect dramatically compared to the whole-Earth scaling.

\[
\Delta a \approx g_s\frac{\Delta m}{M_s} = 10^{-5}\cdot\frac{10}{10^4}=10^{-8}\ \text{m/s}^2,
