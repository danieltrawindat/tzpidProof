\paragraph{Primary Equation.}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{E}:\rho\mapsto \mathcal{E}(\rho).

\paragraph{CPTP Condition.}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{E}\ \text{is completely positive and trace-preserving}.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Primary Derivation.}
Let $\rho$ be a density operator. A quantum channel maps an input state into an output state:
\begin{equation}
\rho'=\mathcal{E}(\rho).
\end{equation}

Physical admissibility requires positivity not only on the original system, but also on any extended system:
\begin{equation}
(\mathcal{E}\otimes I_n)(X)\geq 0 .
\end{equation}

It must also preserve total probability:
\begin{equation}
\operatorname{Tr}(\mathcal{E}(\rho))=\operatorname{Tr}(\rho).
\end{equation}

Thus,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\mathcal{E}:\rho\mapsto\mathcal{E}(\rho)
\quad
\text{is CPTP}.
}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
A quantum channel is the admissible state-transition map for density operators, including TZP-curvature transport corrections.
