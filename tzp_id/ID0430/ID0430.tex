\paragraph{Primary Statement.}

Quantum entanglement between two regions in a Trawinistic manifold can be quantified by the von Neumann entropy.

\paragraph{Primary Derivation.}

For a bipartition of the system into subsystems $A$ and $B$, let $\rho_A = \mathrm{Tr}_B\,\rho$ denote the reduced density matrix of $A$.  The entanglement entropy is

\begin{equation}
S_A = -\mathrm{Tr}\big( \rho_A\log\rho_A \big).
\end{equation}

In Trawin geometry, $S_A$ typically obeys an area law with subleading corrections dependent on the curvature near the zero point.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}

Entanglement reflects topological connections between regions.  At the TZP the area law may receive corrections from the lemniscatic self‑intersection and metric degeneracy.
