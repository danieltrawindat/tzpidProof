\tzpsection{Canonical Statement}
ID 424 establishes the passive stabilization protocol for the Furiene Turbines rotor. By utilizing a Type II high temperature superconductor (HTS) array cooled to 77K, the system induces "Quantum Flux Pinning." This creates a rigid but frictionless magnetic rail that locks the ferrite core into the center of the SS304L torus, ensuring it remains at the exact coordinate of the TZP stagnation point. The "Stiffness" of this rail is vital for maintaining the **Pi Model** synchronization [ID 422]. If the mechanical vibration exceeds the pinning force, the rotor could collide with the vacuum walls. The alignment is cross referenced with the Wwitness to ensure that the "Vibrating Code" of the vacuum manifold does not cause resonant de pinning during high energy extraction. Flux Gapping: Maintains a constant 2mm vacuum gap between the rotor and the torus walls to prevent thermal conduction.
